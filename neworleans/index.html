New Orleans

Louisiana

New Orleans is that rare place where you can order a cocktail to-go, and turn a corner and be swept in a street parade. From the Mardi gras parades to the daily made beignets New Orleans gives a Disneyland feel. The epicenter of jazz musice, and the architecture with french fair, its charms is unlike any other.

Attractions

NEW ORLEANS AREA PLANTATIONS

Much of the south history were based out of these homes. With so many people at one estate it is intresting to see how they live. Some of the Plantations to visit include:

  • St. Joseph Plantation
  • Oak Alley Plantation
  • Nottoway Plantation

FRENCH QUARTER

The French Quater revolves around the Jackson Square since it is named in honor of our President Andrew Jackson. The French Quater is the oldest neighborhood since the French back in the early 18th century. Thing to do may include:

  • Saint Louis Cathedral
  • French Quarter Ghost Tour
  • Presbytere

MUSEUMS

New Orleans has such rich history and many stories. Museums may include:

  • The Cabildo
  • Old Ursuline Convent Museum
  • New Orleans Pharmacy Museum

CEMETERIES

In New Orleans, you'll find cemeteries dating back as early as 1789. Some that you dont want to miss may include:

  • St Roch Cemetery
  • Cypress Grove Cemetery
  • Greenwood Cemetery & Mausoleum

Eateries

Commander’s Palace

Commander's Palace, nestled in the middle of the tree-lined Garden District, has been a New Orleans landmark since 1893.

Herbsaint

Chef Donald Link’s Herbsaint Bar and Restaurant, established in 2000 offering contemporary, seasonal French-Southern cuisine with elements of rustic Italian cooking, locally source.

Coquette

Coquette was opened by Michael in 2008 and offer contemporary southern cuisine with an emphasis on locally sourced product.

Brigtsen’s

This century old-plus Victorian cottage tucked away in the Riverbend is home to some of the best modern Creole fare in the city.

B on Canal is at the center of it all, just steps from The French Quarter, Bourbon Street, theater and business districts with a newly-renovated contemporary and chic outlook on one of the most vibrant cities in the U.S.

The Old No. 77 Hotel & Chandlery is a New Orleans' boutique hotel located just four blocks from the world-famous French Quarter and Bourbon Street and three blocks from the New Orleans Convention Center within easy walking distance of the city's top dining, shopping and nightlife.
